📝 Leadership Agreements & Acknowledgments

Required agreements for all chapter leaders outlining responsibilities, authority, and compliance.

Chapter Leadership Agreement

Purpose: Formal agreement outlining chapter leader responsibilities, nonprofit compliance, brand standards, and removal/enforcement provisions.

Required For: All chapter leaders (must be signed and submitted to FBL)

Key Sections:

  • Role & Authority (stewardship, not ownership)
  • Nonprofit & Legal Compliance (no politics, lobbying, fund misuse)
  • Brand, Culture & Member Experience (no referral promises, no pitch culture)
  • Data, Privacy & Communications (protect member data)
  • Removal & Enforcement (revocable roles)
📜 Download Leadership Agreement (PDF)

Leadership Standards Acknowledgment

Purpose: Acknowledgment confirming chapter leader has read and agrees to uphold FBL brand standards, nonprofit compliance, culture, and conduct requirements.

Required For: All chapter leaders (must be signed and submitted to FBL)

Key Sections:

  • Brand Standards (logo usage, colors, typography, no sub-branding)
  • Chapter Identity (naming standards, messaging rules)
  • Nonprofit Compliance (no politics, lobbying, fund misuse)
  • Communications & Public Representation (professional tone, no hype)
  • Member Data & Privacy (protect member information)
  • Meetings, Culture & Member Experience (no pitch sessions, no quotas)
✍️ Download Leadership Acknowledgment (PDF)

📚 Policies & Compliance Documents

Operational policies ensuring consistent standards and nonprofit compliance across all chapters.

Code of Conduct (Member)

Purpose: Member behavior standards covering culture, respect, confidentiality, nonprofit boundaries, ethics, and enforcement.

Required For: All chapter members (signed during onboarding)

Key Sections:

  1. Culture & Respect (professionalism, time respect, no drama)
  2. No Pitch Culture (no sales sessions, no pressure for referrals)
  3. Confidentiality & Trust (protect private information, no solicitation)
  4. Nonprofit & Public Boundaries (no politics, no controversial representation)
  5. Ethics & Conflicts (integrity, conflict disclosure)
  6. Enforcement (violations may result in removal)
📋 Download Code of Conduct (PDF)

Financial Stewardship Policy

Purpose: Clear requirements for handling money, reimbursements, receipts, approvals, and financial transparency at the chapter level.

Required For: All chapter leaders handling dues, expenses, or financial operations

Key Sections:

  • Stewardship Principles (transparency, accountability, no personal benefit)
  • Handling Funds & Collections (no side accounts, approved processes only)
  • Expenses, Commitments & Approvals (no unauthorized spending)
  • Records, Receipts & Documentation (complete documentation required)
  • Sponsorships, Donations & Partnerships (approval required)
  • Escalation, Reviews & Audit Cooperation (immediate escalation of concerns)
💰 Download Financial Stewardship Policy (PDF)

Communications & Media Policy

Purpose: Standards for communications, social media, public representation, and media/press inquiry handling to protect FBL brand and nonprofit boundaries.

Required For: All chapter leaders with public-facing communications

Key Sections:

  • Communication Standard (professional, no hype, no drama, no politics)
  • Social Media & Public Posts (no political content, no controversial posts)
  • Email, Text Groups & Member Messaging (protect privacy, no solicitation)
  • Public Representation & Spokesperson Rule (only authorized spokespeople)
  • Media / Press Inquiries (escalate immediately, don’t comment)
  • Brand Accuracy, Claims & Partnerships (no false endorsements)
  • Escalation & Enforcement (violations trigger corrective action)
📢 Download Communications & Media Policy (PDF)

Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Purpose: Transparency form for chapter leaders to disclose any personal, financial, professional, or relational interests that could influence FBL decisions.

Required For: All chapter leaders (submitted annually or when conflicts arise)

Key Points:

  • Disclosure does not imply wrongdoing
  • Covers actual, potential, or perceived conflicts
  • Allows FBL to manage risk appropriately
  • Must be updated if new conflicts arise
🔍 Download Conflict of Interest Form (PDF)

Chapter Leader Removal & Succession Policy

Purpose: Policy governing removal, suspension, and succession of chapter leaders to protect mission, nonprofit compliance, brand integrity, and member trust.

Required For: All chapter leaders (awareness and understanding)

Key Sections:

  • Authority (leaders serve at discretion of central leadership)
  • Grounds for Removal (nonprofit violations, brand violations, cultural failures, governance issues)
  • Removal Process (immediate for urgent risks, corrective path when appropriate)
  • Suspension Authority (temporary suspension during review)
  • Succession Protocol (interim leadership, approved successors)
  • Chapter Continuity (chapters belong to FBL, not individual leaders)
  • Communication Standards (professional, confidential transitions)
  • No Entitlement (leadership is service, removal decisions are final)
⚖️ Download Removal & Succession Policy (PDF)

📥 How to Use These Documents

For Prospective Chapter Leaders:

  1. Start with: Operations Guide, Onboarding Checklist, Launch Scorecard
  2. Review: Leadership Agreement, Leadership Acknowledgment
  3. Understand: All policies before applying

For Active Chapter Leaders:

  1. Complete & Submit: Leadership Agreement, Leadership Acknowledgment
  2. Implement: Code of Conduct for all members
  3. Follow: Financial Stewardship, Communications/Media policies
  4. Reference: Operations Guide for ongoing operations
  5. Update: Conflict of Interest disclosure annually

For Chapter Members:

  1. Sign: Code of Conduct during onboarding
  2. Review: Member sections of policies (as applicable)

✉️ Submission Instructions

Required Submissions:

All signed documents should be emailed to: member@fblconnect.com

Subject Line Format: “Chapter Documents - [Chapter Name] - [Document Type]”

Example: “Chapter Documents - FBL Greater Louisville - Leadership Agreement”

Document Retention:

  • FBL maintains records of all signed agreements and acknowledgments
  • Chapter leaders should keep copies for their records
  • Annual renewals or updates will be requested as needed

🔒 Compliance & Standards

These documents exist to:

  • ✅ Protect FBL’s 501(c)(3) nonprofit status
  • ✅ Maintain consistent brand and culture across chapters
  • ✅ Ensure member trust and data privacy
  • ✅ Provide clear expectations and accountability
  • ✅ Create sustainable, high-quality chapters

Non-Negotiables:

  • No political activity, endorsements, or lobbying under FBL
  • No pitch culture or referral promises
  • No brand deviations or sub-branding
  • No misuse of member data
  • No unauthorized financial activity

📞 Questions or Support

If you have questions about any document or need clarification:

Subject: “Chapter Documents Question - [Your Chapter Name]”
