Membership That Produces Real Leverage

FBL membership is for leaders who want a better room—high-trust relationships, real collaboration, and standards that make the network worth something. Not a directory. Not a weekly pitch. A fellowship of operators who already lead.

Why Become a Member?

Most business leaders don’t need more contacts. They need a better table—people they can trust, collaborate with, and grow alongside. Membership is designed to turn relationships into measurable leverage without turning the room into a sales floor.

Trusted Relationships

Build long-term relational equity with leaders who operate with integrity and follow-through.

  • Confidential conversations
  • High-caliber room

Practical Leverage

Strategic introductions, collaborative opportunities, and shared insight from experienced operators.

  • Introductions that matter
  • Partnership-ready peers

Accountability & Growth

A room that respects time and responsibility—and expects you to execute.

  • Clarity and focus
  • Peer-level sharpening

What Being a Member Is Actually About

Membership is a commitment to show up as a leader—bringing value, protecting the culture, and building long-term leverage. The benefit isn’t “access.” The benefit is the room that exists because of shared standards.

  • Contribution-first — you bring value before you ask for it
  • Respect for time — meetings matter, and we don’t waste them
  • Real relationships — built over time, not forced in a single event
  • Standards protected — culture is the product, and we guard it

What Membership Is Not

This is where people self-select. If you want a lead club or recognition without responsibility, you’ll be disappointed.

  • A “pitch circle”
  • Forced referrals or scripts
  • Casual meetups with no standards
  • A directory you pay to access

What the Member Experience Looks Like

FBL is designed to be simple, repeatable, and high-value—so leaders can build leverage without adding chaos to their calendar.

1. Join the Room

Get connected through your local chapter or chapter leadership. Start the membership process to understand the fit and next steps.

2. Build Trust

Relationships come first—leaders learn how each other operate before opportunities ever get discussed.

3. Create Leverage

When the room is strong, introductions, partnerships, and collaboration happen naturally—and actually work.

You’re a Fit If You…

Membership works best for leaders who are contribution-minded and serious about standards.

  • Value trust over transactions
  • Respect time and responsibility
  • Want peers who challenge you
  • Are willing to contribute before you receive
  • Want a better room for the next season

This Is Not For You If You…

No shame—just clarity. The wrong expectations create the wrong room.

  • Need this primarily for leads
  • Want recognition more than responsibility
  • Prefer control over stewardship
  • Want casual networking with no standards
  • Don’t have time to show up consistently

Membership FAQs

Quick answers to the questions serious leaders ask before committing.

Is FBL a lead group?

No. Leads can happen because trust creates opportunity—but FBL is built for relationships, standards, and collaboration. If someone needs structure to be disciplined, there are other groups for that. FBL is for leaders who already lead.

What’s expected of members?

Show up with integrity, respect the room, contribute value, and protect culture. The membership process explains the standards and next steps in detail.

How do I become a member?

Start on the Become a Member page, then follow the instructions to complete the application, assessment, and membership process.

What if I’m not sure I’m a fit?

That’s normal. The fastest clarity comes from reviewing the membership process and comparing the expectations to how you already operate. If you want a better room and you’re willing to contribute to it—you’re likely a fit.
