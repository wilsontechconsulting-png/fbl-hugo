# BYLAWS OF FELLOWSHIP OF BUSINESS LEADERS CORP.
**A Kentucky Nonprofit Corporation**

---

## ARTICLE I — NAME AND PURPOSE

### Section 1. Name
The name of the Corporation is **Fellowship of Business Leaders Corp.** ("the Corporation"), as established in the Articles of Incorporation filed with the Commonwealth of Kentucky.

### Section 2. Mission
Fellowship of Business Leaders unites owners, executives, and professionals dedicated to living out their faith and values through business. Our mission is to inspire, equip, and mobilize leaders for transformative impact.

### Section 3. Purpose
This Corporation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and faith-based purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. No part of the net earnings shall inure to any private individual. No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall consist of carrying on propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the Corporation shall not participate in or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.

---

## ARTICLE II — OFFICES

### Section 1. Principal Office
The principal office of the Corporation shall be **10087 Old Union Road, Union, KY 41091**, or another location as designated by the Board of Directors.

### Section 2. Registered Office
The registered office shall be **9718 Soaring Breezes, Union, KY 41091**, or another address as maintained with the Kentucky Secretary of State.

---

## ARTICLE III — BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 1. General Powers
The Board of Directors ("the Board") shall oversee the affairs, property, and policies of the Corporation.

### Section 2. Number and Composition
The Board shall consist of no fewer than three (3) directors. The initial directors listed in the Articles of Incorporation are:
- John Elvin Heater IV
- Jedediah D. Wilson
- Stephen A. Wright

### Section 3. Qualifications
Directors must share the Corporation's mission, uphold strong ethical standards, and commit to fiduciary responsibilities.

### Section 4. Term of Office
Directors shall serve three-year terms, renewable without limit.

### Section 5. Meetings
Regular meetings shall occur at least twice annually. Special meetings may be called by the Chair or any two directors.

### Section 6. Notice
Written or electronic notice shall be provided at least seven (7) days in advance.

### Section 7. Quorum
A majority of directors then serving constitutes a quorum.

### Section 8. Voting
Actions of the Board require a majority vote of directors present.

### Section 9. Vacancies
The Board may fill vacancies by majority vote.

### Section 10. Removal
A director may be removed by two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board for cause.

---

## ARTICLE IV — OFFICERS

### Section 1. Officers
The Corporation shall have the following officers:
- **Chair**
- **Secretary**
- **Treasurer**

The Board may appoint additional officers as necessary.

### Section 2. Election and Term
Officers are elected annually by the Board.

### Section 3. Duties

**Chair**
- Presides over meetings
- Oversees Board governance
- Represents the Corporation externally

**Secretary**
- Maintains records and minutes
- Manages corporate documents
- Oversees compliance filings

**Treasurer**
- Manages finances, budgets, and reporting
- Ensures IRS and state tax compliance
- Oversees banking and accounting

---

## ARTICLE V — COMMITTEES

### Section 1. Standing Committees
The Board may establish committees such as:
- Executive Committee
- Finance Committee
- Governance Committee
- Program/Leadership Development Committee

### Section 2. Powers
Committees report to the Board and operate under Board-approved charters.

---

## ARTICLE VI — CORPORATE FINANCES

### Section 1. Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall be January 1 through December 31.

### Section 2. Checks and Drafts
All checks, withdrawals, or financial disbursements over $1,000 require dual authorization by any two officers designated by the Board. Disbursements of $1,000 or less may be authorized by any one officer designated by the Board. No individual may authorize a payment to themselves, and all disbursements must comply with the Board-approved budget unless otherwise authorized by the Board.

### Section 3. Deposits
All funds shall be deposited in the Corporation's authorized bank accounts.

---

## ARTICLE VII — CONFLICT OF INTEREST

### Section 1. Policy Required
The Corporation shall maintain a Conflict of Interest Policy consistent with IRS requirements for 501(c)(3) organizations.

### Section 2. Duty to Disclose
Directors and officers must disclose financial or personal interests that may influence decision-making. All directors shall complete and sign an annual Conflict of Interest disclosure form.

### Section 3. Recusal
Any conflicted director must abstain from discussion and voting on the relevant matter.

### Section 4. Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action as determined by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — INDEMNIFICATION

To the fullest extent permitted by Kentucky law, the Corporation shall indemnify any director, officer, employee, or volunteer acting in good faith on behalf of the Corporation.

---

## ARTICLE IX — NONDISCRIMINATION

The Corporation shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, gender, age, disability, or religious affiliation in its programs or activities.

---

## ARTICLE X — AMENDMENTS

These bylaws may be amended by two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board at any regular or special meeting, with at least 10 days' notice.
