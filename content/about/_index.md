## A Better Room for Business Leaders

Fellowship of Business Leaders (FBL) exists to build a stronger room for business leaders across America—one grounded in trust, accountability, and real execution.

Fellowship of Business Leaders is incorporated in Kentucky and recognized by the Internal Revenue Service as a **501(c)(3) public charity**. Contributions are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.

---

## Who We Are

Fellowship of Business Leaders was created for leaders who want more than surface-level conversations and transactional relationships.

- **Builders, founders, and operators**
- **Leaders making real decisions**
- **People who value integrity over image**
- **Individuals seeking real accountability**

---

## Our Story

Fellowship of Business Leaders was formed through the alignment of nonprofit leadership, financial stewardship, and business growth execution.

**John Heater** brings nonprofit leadership and community development.  
**Stephen Wright** brings decades of financial advisory experience and stewardship.  
**Jed Wilson** brings business growth, systems, and execution.

Together, they built FBL to create a high-trust environment where leaders sharpen each other and build meaningful impact.

## Our Vision

Over the next 10 years, FBL is building a national network of leadership communities across counties and cities throughout the United States.

This is not about scale alone—it's about building strong rooms in the right places with the right leaders.
